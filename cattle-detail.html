• Gir cows are a native Indian breed known for their high milk yield.
• A2 milk, disease resistance, and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
• For a dairy farm, key details include an average daily yield of 12–15 liters.
• A lactation period of 280–320 days.
• A total lactation yield of 3,000–4,200 liters with a fat content of 4.5–5.5%.
• They have distinct physical traits like long.
• Folded ears and a prominent hump,
• And their gentle temperament makes them easy to manage.

• Sahiwal farms can be found from small-scale setups to large commercial operations like the Harshit Gupta Dairy Farm in Karnal Haryana.

Tharparkarcow-detail

• Tharparkar cows are found in India.
• Particularly in Rajasthan and Gujarat,
• And are known for their good milk production,
• Averaging 10-15 liters per day under good conditions.
• Some individual cows can produce higher yields,
• With records of up to 16 liters daily and even over 3000 liters per lactation for high-nutrition animals in farm conditions.

Rathi cow-detail

• The Rathi breed is a prominent indigenous Indian cow known for its hardiness and milk production,
• Primarily found in Rajasthan's arid regions.
• Rathi cows typically produce an average of 6 to 8 liters of milk per day,
• Though high-yielding individuals can produce more.
• The milk production of Rathi cows can vary based on factors like feed quality,
• Farm management, and genetics.
