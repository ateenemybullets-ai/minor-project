The World of Small Ruminants

Small ruminants, primarily sheep and goats, are mammals with a unique four-chambered stomach that allows them to digest tough, fibrous plants. They are vital to smallholder farmers, especially in challenging environments, as they efficiently convert low-quality feed into high-quality protein, milk, and fiber with a low initial investment.

Discover Merino Wool

What is Merino Wool?

Merino wool, from the Merino sheep, is considered the highest quality sheep wool available. Its fine, soft structure, with ten times more hairs per square centimeter than average sheep, ensures it doesn't itch.

Organic and Ethical

The Merino wool in the Sophie Stone collection is GOTS-certified organic, sourced from sheep that are not subjected to mulesing, ensuring ethical and sustainable practices.

Key Benefits


  • Temperature Regulating: Protects from both extreme heat and cold.
  • Odor-Resistant:Unlike cotton, it doesn't absorb odors and requires less washing.
  • Moisture-Wicking: Absorbs moisture and wicks it away, keeping you dry.

Discover Suffolk Sheep

What is Suffolk Sheep?

A Suffolk sheep is a British breed of domestic sheep primarily raised for its lean, high-quality meat. Known for its black face and legs, white wool, and polled (hornless) nature, the breed is prized for its rapid growth, early maturity, and adaptability. Suffolk sheep are also frequently used in crossbreeding to produce lambs with enhanced growth and meat traits.

Key characteristics


  • Appearance:They have a black face, ears, and legs, and a white woolly body. Both males (\(rams\)) and females (\(ewes\)) are polled (hornless).
  • Size: Mature rams can weigh between \(250-350\) lbs (\(125\) kg), while ewes weigh between \(180-250\) lbs (\(88\) kg).
  • Temperament:They are known for being docile and easy to manage.Maternal.
  • traits: Suffolk ewes are prolific mothers with good milk production and can often deliver lambs with minimal issues.
  • Origin: The Suffolk breed originated in the East Anglia region of England in the late 1700s from a cross between Norfolk Horn ewes and Southdown rams.
  • Recognition: It was officially recognized as a pure breed in 1810.

Discover Jacob Sheep

What is Jacob Sheep?

A Jacob sheep is a rare, hardy British breed of domestic sheep known for its distinctive appearance, which includes a black and white spotted coat (piebald) and multiple horns, often four. Both males and females have horns, and they can have anywhere from two to six. Historically used as "park ornaments," they are now raised for their unique fleece, meat, and skins, and are considered a heritage breed.

Key characteristics


  • Appearance:They have a piebald coat with patches of black and white, and can have from two to six horns. Horns: Both male and female sheep are horned. Rams typically have larger, more impressive horns than ewes.

Discover Cheviot Sheep

What is Cheviot Sheep?

A Cheviot is a hardy, dual-purpose breed of white-faced sheep that originated in the hills of Scotland and Northumberland, England. Known for their resilience and alert appearance, they are prized for both their high-quality meat and dense wool. They are hornless, have white, wool-free faces and legs, and often have black muzzles and feet

Key characteristics


  • They are known for being hardy and self-sufficient, requiring less husbandry than some other breeds.
  • They are prolific mothers with strong lambing capabilities.
  • Types:
    a) Hill type: Smaller and comparable in size to other hill breeds.
    b) Park type: Larger and more comparable to lowland breeds.
  • Origin: The breed is named after the Cheviot Hills, a range on the border of England and Scotland.
  • Appearance: Cheviots are medium-sized sheep with a distinctive white face and legs that are free of wool. They have alert, erect ears, black muzzles, and black feet.
  • Wool: Their wool is dense, resilient, and has a desirable helical crimp.

Discover Lincoln Sheep

What is Lincoln Sheep?

A Lincoln sheep, or Lincoln Longwool, is a British native breed of large sheep known for producing the heaviest, longest, and most lustrous fleece of any sheep breed. It is a dual-purpose breed, also valued for its substantial carcass, and its wool is used for high-tensile strength products like carpets and wigs.

Key characteristics


  • Size:They are a very large and heavy breed, with mature rams weighing 120–160 kg (265–350 lbs).
  • Wool:Their wool is long (7–8 inches or 175–200 mm), strong, coarse, and very lustrous. The fleece is also one of the heaviest and longest of any breed.
  • Appearance:
    a) Hill type: Smaller and comparable in size to other hill breeds.
    b) Park type: Larger and more comparable to lowland breeds.
  • Origin: The breed is named after the Cheviot Hills, a range on the border of England and Scotland.
  • Appearance: They have a hornless, bold head, and a distinctive forelock of wool around the face. Their face color can be white, blue-grey, or even black.
  • Purpose: They are considered a "dual-purpose" breed, valued for both their wool and meat.
  • Uses: The wool is used for products like upholstery yarns, carpet yarns, and wigs.

Discover Karakul Sheep

What is Karakul Sheep?

A Karakul is a hardy breed of sheep from Central Asia, known for its valuable pelts from young lambs and its coarse wool from adults. These sheep are multi-purpose, raised for their meat, milk, pelts, and wool, and are adaptable to harsh conditions. They are recognized by their distinctive fat-tailed build and the unique, tightly curled, and glossy pelts of their lambs, called Persian lamb in the fur trade

Key characteristics


  • Lamb pelts:The most famous product is the "Persian lamb" pelt from newborn lambs, which have soft, tight, glossy curls, often black.
  • Adult Wool:The wool of adult sheep is coarse and is often used for carpets, outer garments, and felting. Many adult Karakul sheep have a double coat of coarse guard hair and a fine undercoat, which spinners often separate.
  • Meat and milk:
    They are also a source of meat, particularly the fat from their tails, which is an ingredient in some Central Asian cuisines.
  • Hardy and adaptable: Hardy and adaptable: Karakul sheep are known for their hardiness and ability to thrive in rugged environments, making them valuable for pasture improvement.
